FAQs

Frequently asked questions

Can't find what you're looking for? Call or email us directly and our team will help.

Do I need a doctor's referral to see a physiotherapist?

In most cases, no. Physiotherapy is direct-access care, so you can book an assessment with us straight away. If you already have a referral, scan results, or a doctor's report, bring them along — they help us build your plan faster.

What should I wear and bring to my first session?

Loose, comfortable clothing that allows easy access to the area being treated works best — for example shorts for a knee assessment, or a vest top for a shoulder. Bring any relevant medical reports, scans, or referral letters if you have them.

How many physiotherapy sessions will I need?

It depends on your condition, its severity, and how your body responds to treatment. After your first assessment we'll give you a realistic estimate, and we reassess progress at every visit rather than locking you into a fixed package.

Is physiotherapy treatment painful?

Some techniques can bring mild, short-lived discomfort as tight or injured tissue is worked on, but treatment should never be sharply painful. Tell your therapist how something feels during a session — we adjust technique and pressure to what your body can handle.

Do you only treat chronic pain, or sports and post-surgical cases too?

All of the above. Pancare covers the full range of physiotherapy activities — musculoskeletal therapy, sports injury rehabilitation, post-surgical recovery, neurological physiotherapy, posture and ergonomic care, and pain management. See the Services menu for details on each.
